About Me

Some info related to the guy who made this website

My Story

I'm captaincto, a creative developer based in Montréal, Canada. I still have a lot to learn, but two years in I can build real projects from scratch — websites, web apps, interactive tools. I'm passionate about building things that are useful, beautiful, and thoughtfully made.

It all started with Brawl Stars. I got hooked in 2018, started making custom maps for the game, and thought: what if I made my own game? I had zero idea what coding was at the time. But that one question sent me down a rabbit hole that eventually turned into a genuine craft. Seriously — video games have benefits.

After all that brawling, I'm still debugging, coding, and figuring things out. But now I can build things like this portfolio. I can create interactive experiences. Creating projects from scratch takes a lot of time and effort, but seeing something you built actually work? That never gets old.

How I Work

Async by default. I communicate clearly in writing, give proper status updates, and don't need someone to check in every hour to stay on track. Remote work suits me well — I've been working this way from the start.

Self-motivated and independent. Most of my projects were built solo, from idea to deployed product. I'm comfortable figuring things out on my own, reading documentation, and problem-solving without a lifeline.

I ship things. I don't build forever. Projects get finished, pushed, and shared. I care about the quality of what I put out there — clean code, tested features, a product that people can actually use.

What I Value

Quality products. I like to build things that are enjoyable and that can last. If I do something, I'll do it properly. That includes writing clean code, designing user-friendly interfaces, and testing my product many times to make sure it's my best work.

Learning as a lifelong goal. Learning doesn't stop after school. I believe you can learn something new every day. If you think I'm wrong about something, tell me — constructive feedback is always appreciated.

Helping the community. I want my projects to be useful beyond myself — for the people I work with, and the broader community. Giving back matters to me.

Beyond Computers

I like writing and reading about all sorts of things. Trying out activities outside of my comfort zone is always exciting — geography, history, travel, culture, art, music, cooking, acting. A lot of those interests trace back, somehow, to Brawl Stars.

Walking around a new place is one of my favorite things to do. Someday I'd like to visit every continent. But going to Antarctica to see the penguins costs at least $10,000 — which is a lot of V-Bucks. I'll get there eventually.
