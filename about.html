About Me

The story behind the code

My Story

I'm captaincto, a 19-year-old developer who grew up in a city where ancient lantern-lit alleys meet towering neon skyscrapers. This contrast—tradition and innovation, warmth and electricity—has shaped everything about how I see technology and creativity.

My journey into coding started when I was 14, tinkering with HTML and CSS to build fan sites for my favorite games and anime. I didn't have a fancy computer or expensive courses. Just a second-hand laptop, free tutorials, and an unhealthy amount of curiosity. I spent countless nights debugging, googling error messages, and slowly watching things come to life on my screen.

Five years later, I'm still learning—still debugging, still googling, still figuring things out. But now I can build real projects. Apps that people use. Websites that look professional. Code that (usually) works on the first try. I've gone from "What's a div?" to shipping full-stack applications, and it feels like magic every single time.

What I Value

Craft over speed. I'd rather spend an extra hour making something beautiful and thoughtful than rush out something functional but soulless. Every project is a chance to create an experience, not just a product.

Learning over knowing. I'm not afraid to say "I don't know" or "Let me figure that out." Being intermediate means I'm past the basics but far from mastery—and that's exciting. There's so much room to grow.

Culture and code. Technology doesn't exist in a vacuum. The apps we build, the interfaces we design, the stories we tell through our work—they all carry culture, history, and meaning. I want my projects to honor where I come from while pushing toward what's possible.

Beyond Code

When I'm not coding, you'll find me exploring the city at night (there's something about neon signs and empty streets), sketching UI concepts in my notebook, or reading about design systems and creative technology. I'm also learning Japanese, both because I love the language and because so much of the design inspiration I admire comes from Japanese culture.

I believe in building in public, sharing what I learn, and helping others who are where I was a few years ago. That's why I write tutorials, open-source my projects, and try to make the development community a little more welcoming for beginners.
