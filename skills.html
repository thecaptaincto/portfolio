My Approach

I strongly believe in learning by building. Every project is an opportunity to try something new, break something, fix it, and come out stronger. I'm not afraid to dive into unfamiliar territory and ask questions whenever I need help. I highly value efficiency and consistency when it comes to coding.

My coding philosophy is simple: write code that future-you won't hate. Clean, readable, well-commented code isn't just professional — it's respectful to everyone who might work with it, including yourself six months from now. Then you'll have more time to focus on sleeping.

I consider myself at an intermediate level — enough knowledge to cook something powerful, wise enough to know there's always more to learn. I'm comfortable with the fundamentals, passionate about advanced concepts, and always ready to level up.
